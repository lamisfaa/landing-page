# Tutorings Landing Page Project Brief

Source studied: https://tutorings.sa/ and key linked pages on July 12, 2026.

## Current Project Direction

This project is a brand-new lead generation landing page for Tutorings.

The landing page should not be treated as a full redesign of the entire existing Tutorings website. The immediate goal is to create one focused, premium landing page that increases student sign-ups while strengthening trust, brand perception, and awareness of the upcoming English and IELTS practice app.

Do not build the website until the documentation and direction are approved.

## Landing Page Type

Lead Generation Landing Page.

## Primary Goal

Increase student sign-ups.

## Secondary Goals

- Build trust.
- Announce the upcoming English and IELTS practice app.
- Strengthen the Tutorings brand.

## Current Tutorings Offer

Tutorings is an existing education company offering:

- English courses.
- IELTS preparation.
- Private tutors.

An English and IELTS practice app will launch soon. The landing page should introduce the app as an upcoming extension of Tutorings' structured learning experience, not as a replacement for courses or tutors.

## Target Audience For This Landing Page

Primary users:

- Students preparing for university.
- Working professionals who need stronger English for career growth.
- English learners who want structured guidance instead of scattered self-study.

Secondary users:

- IELTS candidates who need focused preparation.
- Learners who have tried multiple resources but still lack a clear path.
- Students comparing Tutorings with self-study, apps, individual tutors, or general online platforms.

## Creative Concept

Many students try to learn English through scattered resources:

- YouTube videos.
- PDFs.
- Mobile apps.
- Notes.
- AI tools.
- Different tutors.
- Random practice materials.

The landing page should communicate the transformation from scattered learning to structured progress.

Core idea:

> Tutorings organizes scattered English-learning resources into one guided learning experience.

The page should visually move from confusion to clarity:

- From many disconnected learning inputs.
- To one organized path.
- To measurable progress with courses, tutors, IELTS support, and the upcoming app.

This transformation should drive the visual story of the page, especially the hero and early sections.

## Visual Direction

Style:

- Premium SaaS.
- White background.
- Tutorings blue accent.
- Dark navy typography.
- Minimal.
- Editorial.
- Modern.

The page should feel calm, sharp, and high-trust. It should not feel like a noisy course marketplace or a traditional education brochure.

Visual inspiration:

- Clutch Security.
- Forah.
- Lusion.

Use these references only for composition, pacing, interaction quality, whitespace, and premium feel. Do not copy layouts, graphics, copy, brand elements, or distinctive visual treatments.

## Visual Storytelling Notes

The landing page should make the "scattered to structured" concept visible.

Possible visual motifs:

- Disconnected resource cards flowing into one organized learning path.
- Messy inputs becoming a clean dashboard or roadmap.
- Notes, videos, PDFs, tutor sessions, app practice, and IELTS tasks converging into one Tutorings system.
- A before/after composition where the left side feels fragmented and the right side feels guided.
- A progress path that connects diagnosis, private tutoring, IELTS practice, app support, and sign-up.

Avoid:

- Generic student stock imagery as the main idea.
- Busy education illustrations.
- Overly playful visuals.
- Decorative gradients that do not support the concept.
- Copying the visual references directly.

## App Announcement Role

The upcoming English and IELTS practice app should be introduced as a future product layer.

It should:

- Build momentum for the brand.
- Show that Tutorings is becoming a broader learning platform.
- Capture interest or waitlist leads if needed.
- Support the main student sign-up goal.

It should not:

- Distract from course and tutor sign-ups.
- Take over the landing page.
- Be positioned as the only product.

Suggested placement:

- Mid-page product preview after the core Tutorings method is explained.
- Or a compact "Coming soon" section before the final CTA.

1. Hero:
   - Clear transformation message.
   - Premium visual showing scattered resources becoming one organized path.
   - Primary CTA for student sign-up or consultation.

6. Upcoming App:
   - English and IELTS practice app coming soon.

   - Optional waitlist or interest CTA.

7. Testimonials / Outcomes:
   - Preserve credible student stories where useful.
   - Prioritize university, career, IELTS, and confidence outcomes.

8. Final CTA:
   - Bring users back to sign-up, consultation, or finding their path.

## Interaction Direction

Interactions should feel premium and purposeful.

Good interaction candidates:

- Hero transformation animation from scattered resources into one path.
- Subtle scroll-based organization of cards into a structured column or roadmap.
- Program cards with restrained hover states.
- App preview with clean product-like motion.
- CTA areas that remain calm and readable.

Avoid interactions that make the page feel like a game, template demo, or animation showcase.
