# Tutorings Landing Page UX And Content Architecture

This document defines the landing page experience before implementation. It is documentation only and should be reviewed before any application code is written.

Project narrative:

1. Hero - communicate the promise of Tutorings.
2. Problem - show how English learning feels scattered and overwhelming.
3. Solution - introduce Tutorings as the structured solution.
4. Learning Process - explain how students progress through Tutorings.
5. Programs - showcase English courses, IELTS preparation, and tutoring.
6. Testimonials - demonstrate real outcomes and build trust.
7. Final CTA - encourage users to start learning.

## 1. Hero

Detailed hero specification is documented in `docs/hero-spec.md`. Use that file as the source of truth for hero layout, headline, visual metaphor, navigation, motion, and trust-logo treatment.

### Section purpose

Immediately communicate Tutorings' core promise: turning scattered English learning into one structured path with courses, IELTS preparation, and private tutors.

### User goal

Understand within seconds whether Tutorings can help them improve English, prepare for IELTS, or find structured guidance.

### Business goal

Capture high-intent leads early through consultation, placement, or sign-up interest.

### Headline direction

The headline should be centered and should communicate the transformation from scattered learning to structured English progress.

Messaging direction:

- Move from random English practice to a clear learning path.
- Learn English with structure, guidance, and the right tutor.
- Turn scattered English resources into real progress.

### Supporting content

Short centered support copy should mention:

- English courses.
- IELTS preparation.
- Private tutors.
- Structured guidance.
- Upcoming English and IELTS practice app as a future layer of support.

Keep this concise. The hero should feel premium, confident, and spacious rather than explanatory.

### Primary CTA

Primary:

- Start Learning

Secondary:

- Book a Consultation

Optional tertiary or lower-emphasis link:

- Join App Waitlist

### Recommended layout

Use a centered hero composition:

- Centered headline.
- Centered supporting copy.
- Centered CTA group.
- Visual system surrounding or sitting below the text, without overpowering the message.

The hero should not use a left-text/right-visual split. The first viewport should feel editorial, minimal, and premium.

### Visual concept

The hero should introduce the "scattered to structured" idea in a refined way. Learning fragments such as videos, PDFs, app practice, notes, AI prompts, tutor sessions, and IELTS tasks can appear as subtle surrounding elements that begin to relate to one central Tutorings path.

The composition should feel organized enough to be premium, but with enough fragmented cues to set up the problem section.

### Interaction or animation ideas

High-level only:

- Subtle entrance where small learning fragments appear around the centered message.
- Light motion suggesting that scattered inputs are beginning to align.
- CTA remains stable and readable.

Avoid loud animation, excessive movement, or anything that makes the hero feel like an animation demo.

### Transition into the next section

The hero should end by hinting that scattered resources are the core pain. The next section expands that tension visually and emotionally.

## 2. Problem

### Section purpose

Show that the issue is not lack of resources, but lack of structure. English learners are surrounded by tools and content, yet still feel unsure what to study next.

### User goal

Feel understood. Recognize their own experience of jumping between videos, PDFs, apps, notes, AI tools, and tutors without clear progress.

### Business goal

Create urgency and make Tutorings' structured approach feel necessary.

### Headline direction

Use one big centered headline.

Messaging direction:

- English learning feels overwhelming when everything is scattered.
- More resources do not always mean clearer progress.
- The problem is not effort. It is the lack of a clear path.

### Supporting content

Keep supporting text minimal in this section. The visual should carry most of the emotional weight.

Supporting ideas:

- Students do not always know their real level.
- Random lessons do not create a structured path.
- Generic apps and videos do not provide personal correction.
- IELTS learners need focused exam preparation, not disconnected practice.
- Different tutors and tools can create confusion without a plan.

### Primary CTA

No primary CTA required in this section.

Optional low-emphasis prompt:

- See how Tutorings fixes this

### Recommended layout

This section should have a Tutorings blue background.

The headline should be large, centered, and visually dominant.

Behind and around the headline, scatter icons and short text fragments representing the student's current learning environment:

- YouTube.
- PDFs.
- Notes.
- Apps.
- AI tools.
- Tutors.
- IELTS tasks.
- Vocabulary lists.
- Grammar rules.
- Exam deadlines.

The scattered items should sit in the background or peripheral areas. They should create the feeling of overload without making the headline hard to read.

### Visual concept

The entire section should feel like a controlled cloud of scattered learning inputs. The blue background creates a strong tonal shift from the white hero and makes the problem feel immersive.

The central headline should remain clear and calm while the surrounding fragments communicate noise, interruption, and lack of order.

### Interaction or animation ideas

High-level only:

- Background icons and text fragments can drift subtly.
- On scroll, scattered elements can move slightly away from the center to keep the headline readable.
- Motion should be slow and premium, not playful.

### Transition into the next section

The section should transition from blue problem space back into clarity. The scattered elements can begin to align or fade as the next headline appears: "Tutorings fixes that."

## 3. Solution

### Section purpose

Introduce Tutorings as the structured solution to the scattered-resource problem.

### User goal

Understand what Tutorings actually does differently.

### Business goal

Position Tutorings above self-study, tutor marketplaces, disconnected apps, and random exam-prep resources.

### Headline direction

The section should start with the headline direction:

> Tutorings fixes that.

The follow-up message should explain that Tutorings organizes English learning around the student's level, goal, tutor, and progress path.

### Supporting content

Explain Tutorings as one guided experience:

- Assess the learner's goal or level.
- Match the student with the right course, IELTS preparation, or private tutor.
- Provide a structured plan instead of random lessons.
- Support progress through teacher guidance, correction, reports, and certificates.
- Extend practice through the upcoming English and IELTS app.

### Primary CTA

- Find Your English Path

Secondary:

- Book a Consultation

### Recommended layout

Use a clean, high-contrast shift from the problem section into a white or very light section.

Recommended composition:

- Strong opening headline: "Tutorings fixes that."
- Short explanatory paragraph.
- Structured visual system showing scattered resources becoming organized under Tutorings.
- Three or four solution pillars, but presented as a flow rather than a feature grid.

### Visual concept

The same scattered elements from the problem section become grouped into a clear Tutorings system:

- English Courses.
- IELTS Preparation.
- Private Tutors.
- Upcoming Practice App.

This section should feel like the moment of clarity after the problem.

### Interaction or animation ideas

High-level only:

- As the section enters, scattered items from the problem section visually resolve into organized categories.
- A clear path or line can connect the categories.
- Motion should reinforce clarity and structure.

### Transition into the next section

After establishing that Tutorings fixes the scattered-learning problem, move into the actual student journey: how progress works step by step.

## 4. Learning Process

Detailed roadmap specification is documented in `docs/roadmap-spec.md`. Use that file as the source of truth for the roadmap structure, milestone content, layout, and scroll behavior before implementation.

### Section purpose

Explain the student journey through Tutorings in a simple, confidence-building way.

### User goal

Know what happens after they show interest or sign up.

### Business goal

Reduce uncertainty and increase lead conversion by making the process feel clear and low-risk.

### Headline direction

A clear path from level check to real progress.

### Supporting content

Suggested process:

1. Identify your level or goal.
2. Choose the right path: English, IELTS, or private tutor.
3. Learn with a structured plan and teacher guidance.
4. Practice, receive feedback, and track progress.
5. Continue with support, certificates, reports, and the upcoming app.

### Primary CTA

- Book a Consultation

Secondary:

- Take the Placement Test

### Recommended layout

Use a journey-based layout rather than feature cards. The process should feel like one continuous progression.

### Visual concept

A single learning line or path moving from unclear level to guided progress. Include subtle references to teacher sessions, reports, IELTS practice, and app practice.

### Interaction or animation ideas

High-level only:

- Steps reveal progressively as the user scrolls.
- The path line can fill gently to reinforce progress.

### Transition into the next section

After explaining the process, introduce the available paths students can choose from.

## 5. Programs

### Section purpose

Present the three core offers clearly: English courses, IELTS preparation, and private tutors.

### User goal

Identify which option fits their current need.

### Business goal

Route users toward the most relevant lead path without overwhelming them.

### Headline direction

Choose the path that matches your goal.

### Supporting content

Program directions:

- English Courses: structured English improvement from beginner to advanced, CEFR-based, private one-to-one lessons, progress reports.
- IELTS Preparation: focused exam preparation, 45-day structure, four skills, correction, strategy, IELTS-specialized teachers.
- Private Tutors: personalized guidance, bilingual support, flexible scheduling, fixed teacher continuity.

The upcoming app can be lightly referenced as practice support, but should not overpower the three core offers.

### Primary CTA

Each program can have a contextual CTA:

- Improve My English
- Prepare for IELTS
- Find a Private Tutor

### Recommended layout

Use three premium program panels. They should be visually related but distinct by goal. Prioritize audience and outcome over long feature lists.

### Visual concept

Each program appears as a structured route inside the Tutorings system. English is the foundation path, IELTS is the exam-focused path, tutoring is the personalized support path.

### Interaction or animation ideas

High-level only:

- Restrained hover or focus states.
- Reveal concise details such as duration, format, outcome, or next step.

### Transition into the next section

After the user sees the options, show proof that these paths produce real outcomes.

## 6. Testimonials

Detailed testimonials carousel specification is documented in `docs/testimonials-spec.md`. Use that file as the source of truth for testimonial content, carousel behavior, controls, and accessibility before implementation.

### Section purpose

Build trust through real learner outcomes.

### User goal

See evidence that students like them improved, passed exams, or gained confidence.

### Business goal

Reduce skepticism and reinforce credibility before the final CTA.

### Headline direction

Real students. Clearer progress.

### Supporting content

Use selected testimonials from the content inventory:

- Mohammad Al-Turki: improved speaking with foreign clients after General English.
- Yaser Al Qahtani: joined IELTS for scholarship needs and achieved 7/9.
- Renad Al Ghamdi: moved from level 1 to level 3 and improved speaking with colleagues.

Optional STEP score examples can be used if STEP remains part of the landing page story.

Support testimonials with trust signals nearby:

- 50+ teachers.
- 200+ students.
- Bilingual teachers.
- IDP partnership.
- Cambridge One.
- Trustpilot.
- Tabby/Tamara.

### Primary CTA

Optional:

- Start Your Path

### Recommended layout

Use an editorial testimonial section with two or three strong stories first, followed by a compact trust strip. Avoid a crowded review wall.

### Visual concept

Outcome-focused cards: student goal, Tutorings path, result. Make the story feel human and measurable.

### Interaction or animation ideas

High-level only:

- Subtle carousel or staggered reveal.
- Keep trust content stable and readable.

### Transition into the next section

Move from proof to action: now choose your starting point.

## 7. Final CTA

### Section purpose

Convert the user after the full story: confusion, structure, process, options, proof.

### User goal

Know exactly what to do next.

### Business goal

Capture the lead.

### Headline direction

Start with the path that fits your goal.

### Supporting content

Reassure users that they do not need to know the perfect program yet. Tutorings can help them choose through consultation, placement, or program selection.

### Primary CTA

- Book a Consultation

Secondary:

- Take the Placement Test

Optional lower-emphasis:

- Join App Waitlist

### Recommended layout

Clean, focused final section with one dominant action. Use minimal supporting text and a calm trust reminder.

### Visual concept

The final state of the story: scattered learning has become one clear Tutorings path. This should echo the hero, but now resolved and simplified.

### Interaction or animation ideas

High-level only:

- Light path-completion animation.
- Subtle CTA emphasis.
- Keep the section conversion-focused.

### Transition out

No major new information after this. Footer should be minimal and should not dilute the lead-generation goal.
